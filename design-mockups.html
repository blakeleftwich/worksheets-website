Summary: The Dual-Audience Strategy

Here's how the best educational sites handle the teacher-vs-kid balance:

1. One consistent header/nav — The brand identity (logo, navigation) stays the same everywhere. This is the "trust anchor."

2. White/clean for educator pages — Worksheets, tools, and generator pages use white backgrounds and clean sans-serif type. Professional = trustworthy.

3. Dark/colorful for game pages — Games switch to dark backgrounds, vibrant gradients, and playful card art. Kids instantly feel the shift to "play mode."

4. Trust signals everywhere — "100% Free", "No Sign-Up Required", "Answer Keys Included", grade badges, and Common Core alignment badges.

5. Category-first navigation — Organize by math topic, not by content type. Let worksheets, tools, and games live under the same topic umbrella.

6. Type badges on cards — Small tags like "Worksheet", "Tool", "Game" on each card let users know what they're getting.
